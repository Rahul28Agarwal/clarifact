How to detect fake news?

1. Consider the Source

News stories or articles from well-know news outlets such as ABC news, world health organisation, world trade organisation, 9news, Sydney morning herald etc are far more reliable than sites such as Facebook, WhatsApp university, twitter etc.

Learn More

2. Check the Author

Check the author’s bio and do a quick search on them. Are they credible to write about their story? Are they real? Are they experienced?
Trust the author before you trust the news!

Learn More

3. Check the Date

"Change is the only constant". What news might have been correct at some point of time, might be fake in today’s time! It is important to stay updated with the correct version of news.

Learn More

4. Check for Biases

Especially with the rise of opinionated journalism and websites profiting from polarization, consider the intended audience for this story and if your own beliefs could affect your judgement.

Learn More

5. Read Beyond

Even legitimate news sources will sometimes use misleading headlines. As you're reading the article, ask yourself if the content matches the headline. If there are clues whether an article is fake or not, there are going to be more of them in the body of the article, not the title. Read beyond the headline and decide for yourself!

Learn More

6. Supporting Sources

As quoted by Jolene Engle, “For Two are Better than One, but Three is Even Better”. Multiple sources provide higher scrutiny and investigation of news. It helps improve in-depth knowledge. More research lets one explore from different angles and perspectives.

Learn More

7. Ask the Experts

Supplement your research with an expert advice from those who know the best. Ask questions that have not been answered. Seek knowledge from trustworthy organisations and respective intellectuals.
